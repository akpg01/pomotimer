  1. Choose a task to be accomplished.
  2. Set the Pomodoro timer (25-minutes).
  3. Work on the task until the timer rings.
  4. Take a short break (5-minutes)
  5. After 4 Pomodoros, take a long break (15-minutes)

The Pomodoro Technique was invented in the early 90s by developer, entrepeneur, and author Francesco Cirillo. He named the system "Pomodoro" after a tomato-shaped timer he used to track his work during his time as university student. Click here to learn more about Francesco Cirillo and the Pomodoro Technique!
